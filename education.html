Education: Learn About Diseases

Diabetes

A metabolic disease that causes high blood sugar due to inadequate insulin production or response.

Hypertension

A condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high, potentially leading to heart disease.

Asthma

A chronic disease of the airways that makes breathing difficult due to inflammation and narrowing of the airways.

Cancer

A group of diseases involving abnormal cell growth with the potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body.

Alzheimer's Disease

A progressive neurological disorder that causes brain cells to waste away and die, leading to memory loss and cognitive decline.

Arthritis

A condition causing inflammation and pain in the joints, which can affect one or multiple joints.

Coronary Heart Disease

A disease caused by the buildup of plaque in the coronary arteries, which supply blood to the heart, leading to heart attacks.

Obesity

A complex disease involving an excessive amount of body fat, increasing the risk of other diseases and health problems.

Epilepsy

A neurological disorder where brain activity becomes abnormal, causing seizures or periods of unusual behavior.

Depression

A mental health disorder characterized by persistently depressed mood or loss of interest in activities, affecting daily life.

Anxiety Disorders

A group of mental health disorders characterized by significant feelings of anxiety and fear.

Hepatitis

Inflammation of the liver commonly caused by viral infections, leading to liver damage and possibly liver failure.

Malaria

A disease caused by a parasite transmitted through the bite of infected mosquitoes, causing fever, chills, and flu-like symptoms.

Pneumonia

An infection of the lungs that can cause mild to severe symptoms like cough, fever, and difficulty breathing.

Stroke

A condition where blood flow to a part of the brain is interrupted, causing brain cells to die due to lack of oxygen.

Chronic Kidney Disease

A long-term condition where the kidneys do not work effectively, possibly leading to kidney failure over time.

Bronchitis

Inflammation of the bronchial tubes, causing cough and mucus production, often due to infection or smoking.

Anemia

A condition in which there is a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin, leading to fatigue and weakness.

Chickenpox

A contagious viral infection characterized by an itchy rash and red spots or blisters all over the body.

Measles

A highly contagious viral disease that causes fever, cough, and a characteristic red rash.

Whooping Cough (Pertussis)

A bacterial respiratory disease causing severe coughing spells and a whooping sound when inhaling.

Osteoporosis

A disease that weakens bones, making them more susceptible to sudden and unexpected fractures.

Gout

A type of arthritis caused by the accumulation of uric acid crystals in the joints, leading to intense pain and swelling.

Eczema

A condition that causes inflamed, itchy, cracked, and rough skin, often in response to an allergen or irritant.

Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

A disease where the immune system attacks the protective covering of nerves, leading to nerve damage and disability.

Crohn's Disease

A chronic inflammatory bowel disease that affects the lining of the digestive tract, causing pain and digestive issues.

Ulcerative Colitis

A chronic inflammatory bowel disease that causes inflammation and sores in the digestive tract, particularly the colon and rectum.

Psoriasis

An autoimmune disease that causes rapid skin cell production, leading to scaling and inflamed, red patches on the skin.

Hepatitis B

A viral infection that causes liver inflammation, potentially leading to liver failure or cancer if chronic.

Hepatitis C

A viral infection causing liver inflammation, often resulting in chronic liver disease, cirrhosis, or liver cancer.

Dengue Fever

A mosquito-borne viral infection causing fever, headache, muscle pain, and rash, which can become life-threatening.

Yellow Fever

A viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes, causing fever, chills, jaundice, and potentially liver and kidney failure.

Zika Virus

A mosquito-borne virus that can cause mild symptoms but is linked to birth defects in babies born to infected mothers.

Leukemia

A type of cancer affecting blood and bone marrow, leading to abnormal white blood cell production and blood-related symptoms.

Lymphoma

A group of blood cancers that affect the lymphatic system, causing swollen lymph nodes, fever, and fatigue.

Melanoma

A serious form of skin cancer arising from pigment-producing cells, often due to UV exposure, with potential to spread if untreated.

Eczema

A skin condition causing itchy and inflamed patches of skin, often triggered by environmental factors or allergens.

Herpes

A viral infection causing painful sores, often on the mouth (HSV-1) or genital area (HSV-2); it remains in the body for life.

Scabies

A contagious skin infestation by mites, causing intense itching and a rash, often transmitted through close physical contact.

Hyperthyroidism

A condition where the thyroid gland produces too much hormone, leading to rapid metabolism, weight loss, and nervousness.

Hypothyroidism

A condition in which the thyroid gland produces too little hormone, leading to fatigue, weight gain, and slowed metabolism.

Albinism

A genetic condition resulting in little to no production of melanin, causing pale skin, light hair, and vision problems.

Vitiligo

A condition causing loss of skin pigmentation in patches due to the destruction of pigment-producing cells.

Epilepsy

A neurological disorder where brain activity becomes abnormal, leading to seizures or periods of unusual behavior.

Narcolepsy

A chronic sleep disorder characterized by overwhelming daytime drowsiness and sudden attacks of sleep.

Polio (Poliomyelitis)

A viral disease that can cause paralysis, muscle weakness, and sometimes fatal respiratory issues.

Rubella

A contagious viral infection characterized by a red rash and mild fever, dangerous for pregnant women due to risk of birth defects.

Tetanus

A serious bacterial infection affecting the nervous system, leading to painful muscle stiffness and spasms.

HIV/AIDS

A virus that attacks the body's immune system, leading to acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS) and increased vulnerability to infections.

Cystic Fibrosis

A genetic disorder affecting the lungs and digestive system, characterized by thick, sticky mucus production leading to respiratory issues.

Type 1 Diabetes

A chronic condition where the pancreas produces little or no insulin, leading to high blood sugar levels.

Type 2 Diabetes

A chronic condition affecting the way the body processes blood sugar (glucose), often associated with obesity and insulin resistance.

Hypertension

A condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high, increasing the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Asthma

A chronic lung disease that inflames and narrows the airways, causing wheezing, shortness of breath, and chest tightness.

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

A progressive lung disease that obstructs airflow and makes it difficult to breathe, often caused by smoking.

Heart Disease

A range of conditions affecting the heart, including coronary artery disease and heart attack, often linked to lifestyle factors.

Liver Disease

A general term for conditions that damage the liver, leading to inflammation, scarring (cirrhosis), and potential liver failure.

Kidney Disease

A condition where the kidneys lose their ability to filter waste and excess fluids from the blood, leading to kidney failure.

Gout

A form of arthritis characterized by sudden, severe pain, redness, and swelling in joints, often caused by high uric acid levels.

Chronic Fatigue Syndrome

A complex disorder characterized by extreme fatigue that lasts for at least six months and is not improved with rest.

Autoimmune Diseases

Conditions where the immune system mistakenly attacks the body, affecting various organs and tissues, leading to inflammation and damage.

Migraines

Severe headaches often accompanied by nausea and sensitivity to light and sound, triggered by various factors.

Pneumonia

An infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, causing cough, fever, and difficulty breathing.

Hemochromatosis

A genetic disorder where the body absorbs too much iron, which can accumulate in organs, causing damage.

Osteomyelitis

An infection in a bone, often caused by bacteria entering through the bloodstream or from an injury.

Guillain-Barré Syndrome

An autoimmune disorder where the immune system attacks the peripheral nerves, causing weakness and potential paralysis.

Whooping Cough (Pertussis)

A highly contagious respiratory infection causing severe coughing fits and characteristic "whooping" sounds.

Ebola Virus Disease

A severe, often fatal disease caused by the Ebola virus, leading to hemorrhagic fever, organ failure, and bleeding.

Psoriasis

An autoimmune condition causing red, scaly skin patches, often on the elbows, knees, scalp, and lower back.

Celiac Disease

An autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine.

Aspergillosis

A fungal infection affecting the lungs, primarily impacting individuals with weakened immune systems or lung diseases.

Beriberi

A condition caused by a deficiency in vitamin B1 (thiamine), leading to cardiovascular and nervous system issues.

Scurvy

A disease caused by vitamin C deficiency, leading to anemia, gum disease, and skin hemorrhages.

Rickets

A condition in children caused by vitamin D deficiency, leading to soft and weakened bones.

HIV/AIDS

A virus that attacks the immune system, leading to a progressive immune deficiency and increased susceptibility to infections.

Toxoplasmosis

An infection caused by a parasite found in cat feces and contaminated food, particularly dangerous for pregnant women and immunocompromised individuals.

Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease (CJD)

A rare, degenerative, and fatal brain disorder caused by prion proteins, leading to rapid neurological decline.

Measles

A highly contagious viral infection causing fever, cough, and a distinctive rash; preventable by vaccination.

Mumps

A viral infection that primarily affects the salivary glands, causing swelling, pain, and fever.

Schizophrenia

A chronic mental disorder involving delusions, hallucinations, and impaired thinking.

Parkinson's Disease

A neurodegenerative disorder causing tremors, rigidity, and impaired movement due to the loss of dopamine-producing cells.

Huntington's Disease

A genetic disorder causing progressive breakdown of nerve cells in the brain, leading to movement, cognitive, and psychiatric disorders.

Muscular Dystrophy

A group of genetic disorders characterized by progressive muscle weakness and degeneration.

Marfan Syndrome

A genetic disorder affecting connective tissue, leading to long limbs and fingers, as well as potential heart issues.

Down Syndrome

A genetic condition caused by an extra chromosome 21, leading to developmental and intellectual disabilities.

Angelman Syndrome

A genetic disorder affecting the nervous system, causing developmental delays, seizures, and happy demeanor.

Williams Syndrome

A rare genetic disorder characterized by cardiovascular issues, unique facial features, and an overly friendly personality.

Asperger's Syndrome

A developmental disorder on the autism spectrum affecting social interaction and communication skills.

Hemophilia

A genetic disorder affecting blood clotting, leading to excessive bleeding from even minor injuries.

Anaphylaxis

A severe, life-threatening allergic reaction causing swelling, difficulty breathing, and a drop in blood pressure.

Graves' Disease

An autoimmune disorder that causes hyperthyroidism, leading to an overactive thyroid gland and accelerated metabolism.

Lupus

A chronic autoimmune disease that causes inflammation in various body parts, including skin, joints, and organs.

Sarcoma

A type of cancer that originates in bones and soft tissues, including muscles, fat, and blood vessels.

Lyme Disease

A bacterial infection spread by tick bites, causing skin rashes, joint pain, and potentially affecting the heart and nervous system.

Yellow Fever

A viral disease transmitted by mosquitoes, affecting the liver and causing fever, chills, and jaundice.

Leprosy (Hansen's Disease)

A chronic bacterial infection affecting the skin, nerves, and mucous membranes, leading to skin lesions and nerve damage.

Rabies

A deadly viral infection transmitted by animal bites, affecting the central nervous system and causing neurological symptoms.

Tuberculosis (TB)

A bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs, causing coughing, fever, and weight loss; spread through airborne droplets.

Cholera

An acute diarrheal illness caused by ingestion of Vibrio cholerae bacteria, leading to severe dehydration and, if untreated, death.

Typhoid Fever

A bacterial infection caused by Salmonella typhi, spread through contaminated food and water, causing high fever and abdominal pain.

Anthrax

A serious bacterial disease caused by Bacillus anthracis, spread by spores, affecting skin, lungs, or digestive system.

Brucellosis

A bacterial infection from livestock, leading to fever, joint pain, and excessive sweating; transmitted through infected animal products.

Leptospirosis

A bacterial disease spread through animal urine, affecting the liver and kidneys and leading to fever, chills, and headache.

Legionnaires' Disease

A severe form of pneumonia caused by Legionella bacteria, typically spread through contaminated water sources.
